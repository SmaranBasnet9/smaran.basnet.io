Microsoft Unveils In-House AI Models to Cut OpenAI Reliance

June 2, 2026 Summarized by Smaran

Microsoft introduced two new in-house models at its Build developer conference: MAI-Code-1-Flash, its first coding-focused model, and MAI-Thinking-1, a reasoning model.

The headline here isn't the models themselves so much as what they signal about Microsoft's strategy: after years of leaning heavily on OpenAI for the AI features baked into its developer tools and products, Microsoft is now shipping its own models for at least some of that workload.

Two practical reasons that matters. First, cost — running your own model for a chunk of traffic is cheaper than paying another company per token at scale. Second, control — relying entirely on a single outside vendor for a core product capability is a risk Microsoft has clearly decided to start hedging against, even while remaining a major OpenAI investor and partner.

For teams building on Microsoft's stack, this is worth tracking less as "which model is smarter" and more as a sign that the underlying model powering a given feature may start switching between vendors depending on cost and task — which can quietly change output quality or behavior even when nothing in your own setup changed.
